# Geographic Dispersal & Secondary Cluster Locations

Following the [[02-mv-hondius-outbreak-vector|MV Hondius outbreak]], passengers and crew dispersed to multiple countries, creating a complex international contact-tracing challenge.

## Key Dispersal Events

1. **Saint Helena (Apr 24)** — Adult female disembarked with GI symptoms; deteriorated during flight to Johannesburg on Apr 25; died at ER on Apr 26. First fatality outside the ship.

2. **Tristan da Cunha** — British military parachuted in to assist; one of the island's ~220 residents (former ship passenger) suspected of infection. Remote island complicates evacuation.

3. **Six European countries + Canada** — Bulk repatriation flights departed after ship arrival on May 10.

## Case Tally (May 12)

- **Confirmed:** 9
- **Suspected:** 2  
- **Deaths:** 3 (2 confirmed Andes virus)

## Contact-Tracing Implications

See [[contact_tracing_methodologies_and_limitations]] for challenges. Multi-country dispersal requires coordination across:
- CDC (US monitoring)
- ECDC (European cluster)
- WHO (global coordination)
- National health agencies in each repatriation country
