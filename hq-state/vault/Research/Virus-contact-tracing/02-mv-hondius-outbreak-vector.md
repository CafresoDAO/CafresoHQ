# MV Hondius Identified as Outbreak Vector

The 2026 hantavirus outbreak has been traced to the Dutch cruise ship, the MV Hondius. International health organizations, including the WHO, CDC, and ECDC, confirmed a cluster of cases among passengers in April and May of 2026.

- **Virus Strain:** The outbreak was caused by the Andes virus, a strain of hantavirus known for its rare potential for human-to-human transmission.
- **Timeline:** The first cases were identified in April 2026, with official health alerts appearing in early May.
- **Initial Impact:** The initial cluster included nine confirmed cases, two suspected cases, and three deaths.
