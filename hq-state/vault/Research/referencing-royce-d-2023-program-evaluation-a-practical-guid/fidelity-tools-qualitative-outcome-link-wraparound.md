# Fidelity Tools as Qualitative Outcome Bridges in Wraparound Evaluation

## Core Argument
Royce (2023) emphasizes in **Chapter 8 (Qualitative Approaches to Evaluation)** that qualitative data collection must be purposefully structured to capture program mechanisms — not just final outcomes. Fidelity instruments like the **Wraparound Fidelity Index (WFI)** and its short form, the **WFI-EZ**, operationalize this principle by blending adherence measurement with client-reported qualitative outcomes.

## The Wraparound Fidelity Index (WFI / WFI-EZ)
- Developed by the **Wraparound Evaluation and Research Team (WERT)** at University of Washington
- Measures adherence to wraparound principles across four respondent groups: **facilitators, caregivers, youth (11+), and team members**
- WFI-EZ includes items directly tracking **perceived outcomes** (e.g., "home life improved since starting Wraparound") alongside fidelity indicators
- Can be administered as a **structured interview** — a format Royce (**Ch. 8**) identifies as a key qualitative data collection method that allows probing and narrative capture

## Why This Matters for Qualitative Outcome Evaluation
Royce (**Ch. 7, Outcome Evaluation**) distinguishes between *output* (services delivered) and *outcome* (change in client condition). The WFI bridges this:

| WFI Function | Royce Alignment |
|---|---|
| Fidelity ratings by family/youth | Formative qualitative data (Ch. 8) |
| Outcome self-report items | Summative outcome data (Ch. 7) |
| Multi-informant design | Triangulation principle (Ch. 8) |
| Statewide aggregate use | Program-level evaluation (Ch. 3) |

## Implementation Fidelity as a Causal Qualifier
Attell et al. (2023, *Evaluation and Program Planning*) found that fidelity scores predict outcome trajectories — a finding directly applicable to Royce's (**Ch. 9**) discussion of **rival explanations and internal validity**. High fidelity scores allow evaluators to rule out "poor implementation" as an alternative explanation when outcomes fall short.

Duration note: families typically need **10–18 months** of high-fidelity wraparound for sustainable outcomes (Miles et al., 2019) — reinforcing the longitudinal design argument in [[longitudinal-qualitative-tracking-wraparound]].

## Qualitative Depth Beyond the Survey
The WFI alone is quantitative. Royce (**Ch. 8**) would push evaluators to supplement it with:
- **Focus groups** with team members after fidelity reviews
- **Document analysis** of care plans and team meeting notes (also used by external evaluators per WERT quality assurance tools)
- **Member-checking** — sharing fidelity results back with families to validate interpretations
