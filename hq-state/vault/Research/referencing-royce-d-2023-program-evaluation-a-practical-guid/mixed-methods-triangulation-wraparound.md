# Mixed-Methods Triangulation in Wraparound Evaluation

Royce (2023) presents mixed-methods design in Ch. 9 as a deliberate strategy for capturing dimensions of program performance that neither quantitative nor qualitative approaches can address alone. Ch. 10 extends this by framing triangulation — using multiple data sources, methods, or investigators to converge on findings — as a central criterion for rigor in applied program evaluation. Together, these chapters provide direct methodological grounding for wraparound evaluators seeking to connect fidelity measurement to long-term family and youth outcomes.

## Why Mixed Methods for Wraparound

High-fidelity wraparound is defined by structured adherence to its ten principles, yet the mechanisms by which that adherence translates into durable outcomes remain difficult to capture with numbers alone. Quantitative fidelity scores (e.g., Wraparound Fidelity Index, WFI-4) document whether practices occurred; qualitative narratives from youth and families explain *why* those practices did or did not matter in context.

- Royce Ch. 9 distinguishes concurrent, sequential, and embedded mixed-methods designs — all three apply to wraparound evaluation depending on study phase and resource constraints
- Royce Ch. 10 identifies triangulation as validation through convergence: when qualitative themes align with high fidelity scores and positive outcome data, confidence in causal inference increases substantially

## Concurrent QUAL + QUAN Structures

PMC6501787 demonstrates a concurrent structure pairing measurement-based care (MBC) fidelity data with qualitative interviews about implementation barriers. In this design, both strands are collected simultaneously and integrated at the interpretation stage. For wraparound programs, this means:

- Running WFI-4 or similar tools in parallel with structured family narrative interviews
- Merging findings to identify which fidelity domains are most influenced by organizational or family-level barriers surfaced in qualitative data

PMC3025112 reinforces this approach by using qualitative data for contextual richness (team dynamics, community fit) while quantitative data addresses fidelity content and standardized outcome scores.

## Sequential and Longitudinal Mixed-Methods

PMC3841106 (SafeCare study) applies both concurrent and sequential mixed-methods designs across a longitudinal fidelity monitoring protocol. In sequential designs, one strand informs the other across time — qualitative findings from early implementation refine the questions asked quantitatively in later phases. For wraparound evaluators:

- Baseline qualitative interviews (family goals, perceived barriers) can shape which outcome domains are prioritized in quantitative tracking
- See [[longitudinal-qualitative-tracking-wraparound]] for sustained data collection protocols across the wraparound plan lifecycle

## Triangulation as Rigor

A ScienceDirect study on multi-component family support programs identifies complementary qual/quant procedures as essential when interventions involve multiple active components — exactly the structure of wraparound, which operates across service, relational, and systemic domains simultaneously.

Royce Ch. 10 frames triangulation rigor around four tests: credibility, transferability, dependability, and confirmability. In practice:

- **Credibility**: Qualitative themes are validated against fidelity subscale scores (see [[fidelity-tools-qualitative-outcome-link-wraparound]])
- **Transferability**: Rich qualitative description of context enables comparison across sites
- **Dependability**: Audit trails for both data strands support replication
- **Confirmability**: Independent review of qualitative coding checked against quantitative patterns reduces evaluator bias

## Practical Implications for Wraparound Evaluators

1. Design for integration from the outset — data collection instruments for both strands should share conceptual anchors (e.g., the ten wraparound principles)
2. Use family and youth voice as the qualitative strand, not solely staff reports (see [[participatory-youth-family-voice-wraparound]])
3. Report triangulated findings together in evaluation briefs — separate reporting of fidelity scores and narrative summaries undermines the rigor that triangulation is meant to provide
4. Connect triangulated findings to the [[qualitative-outcome-measurement-wraparound]] and [[qualitative-outcome-measurement-hfw-overview]] frameworks to maintain alignment with broader outcome evaluation goals
