# 08: Vaccine Development Status

As of 2026, there is no approved vaccine available for the Andes virus. This is a significant factor in managing outbreaks, as noted in the [[01-initial-outbreak-summary]], and places greater emphasis on [[06-prevention-and-control-measures]].

However, research is active and several vaccine candidates are in development. Key advancements include:

- **Phase I Clinical Trials:** A DNA vaccine for the Andes virus has undergone initial Phase I clinical trials to test for safety and its ability to provoke an immune response.
- **Animal Models:** A crucial breakthrough has been the development of hamster models that accurately replicate human Hantavirus Pulmonary Syndrome (HPS). These models are essential for testing the effectiveness of vaccines and therapeutics before human trials.
- **Ongoing Efforts:** Multiple research groups and companies, including Moderna, are reportedly working on hantavirus vaccines, though none are yet approved for public use.
