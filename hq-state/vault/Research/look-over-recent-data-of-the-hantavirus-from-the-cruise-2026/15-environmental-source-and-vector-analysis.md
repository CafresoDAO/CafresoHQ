# 15 - Environmental Source and Vector Analysis

Investigations into the 2026 hantavirus outbreak on the MV Hondius did not identify a rodent vector population aboard the vessel. The prevailing hypothesis, supported by the World Health Organization (WHO), is that the index case acquired the Andes virus through environmental exposure in Argentina *before* boarding the cruise.

This finding is critical because it shifts the focus from a ship-wide environmental contamination issue to the specific circumstances of the initial patient's exposure. It also underscores that the cruise ship served as the amplifier for the outbreak primarily through [[02-andes-virus-transmission|person-to-person transmission]], a known feature of the Andes virus strain.

Consequently, the primary [[07-public-health-response-and-epidemiology|public health response]] focused on contact tracing and quarantine of passengers and crew rather than extensive pest control measures on the ship itself. The outbreak was a cluster of human-to-human cases originating from a single zoonotic spillover event that occurred off the ship.
