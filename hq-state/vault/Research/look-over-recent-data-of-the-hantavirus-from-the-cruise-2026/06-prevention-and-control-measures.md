# 06: Prevention and Control Measures

Public health authorities like the WHO, ECDC, and CDC responded to the [[01-initial-outbreak-summary|2026 cruise ship outbreak]] with a multi-faceted strategy to limit further spread of the Andes virus. Given the potential for [[02-andes-virus-transmission|human-to-human transmission]] and the virus's long incubation period, these measures were critical.

Key interventions included:

*   **On-board Measures:** Immediate actions were taken on the ship to reduce infection likelihood among remaining passengers and crew.
*   **Isolation and Quarantine:** Symptomatic individuals were immediately isolated, and quarantine protocols were established for those exposed.
*   **Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):** Universal respirator use was enforced, particularly during disembarkation. Healthcare workers providing direct care were also required to use respirators.
*   **Ventilation:** Efforts were made to improve air circulation, such as conducting health screenings outdoors and using open-window transport where possible.
*   **Contact Tracing and Follow-up:** Authorities initiated comprehensive contact tracing, testing, and follow-up procedures for all passengers to catch new cases early after they had disembarked.
