# 16. International Repatriation and Quarantine Protocols

The 2026 Hantavirus cruise ship outbreak triggered a major international response focused on the safe repatriation and quarantine of passengers and crew. Health organizations like the WHO, CDC, and ECDC coordinated efforts, as detailed in the [[07-public-health-response-and-epidemiology|public health response summary]].

Key aspects of the protocol included:

*   **Recommended Quarantine Period:** The World Health Organization (WHO) recommended a 42-day (six-week) quarantine period for all disembarked individuals to monitor for the development of symptoms.
*   **Coordinated Repatriation:** Passengers were flown home on military and government-chartered aircraft to various countries, including the US and Australia.
*   **Dedicated Facilities:** Upon arrival in their home countries, passengers were often housed in dedicated medical or quarantine facilities for the duration of the monitoring period.

While post-disembarkation procedures are well-documented, specific details regarding the initial [[15-environmental-source-and-vector-analysis|shipboard quarantine]] and decontamination protocols are less available in public sources. This suggests a focus on moving passengers to more controlled land-based facilities as quickly as was feasible.
