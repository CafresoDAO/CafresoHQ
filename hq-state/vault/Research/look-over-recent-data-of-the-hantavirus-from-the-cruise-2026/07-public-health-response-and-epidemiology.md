# Public Health Response and Epidemiology

The 2026 Andes virus outbreak on the MV Hondius cruise ship triggered a multi-national public health response coordinated by the World Health Organization (WHO), with involvement from the CDC and ECDC.

## Epidemiological Investigation
Key epidemiological efforts focused on identifying the source and containing the spread:
- **Source Tracing:** The Argentine health ministry and the Malbrán Institute led investigations into the index case's travel history. This involved capturing and testing rodents along the route the initial patient traveled in South America before boarding the ship.
- **Contact Tracing:** Authorities initiated contact tracing for passengers and crew to identify further cases and prevent secondary transmission, as detailed in the [[01-initial-outbreak-summary]].
- **Virus Identification:** The WHO officially confirmed the causative agent as the Andes virus on May 6, 2026, which was crucial for understanding the [[02-andes-virus-transmission]] dynamics, particularly the risk of person-to-person spread in the ship's close quarters.

## Containment and Management
The WHO issued technical guidance for managing the situation on board the ship and for the safe disembarkation and onward management of all individuals. The CDC also provided situation summaries, assessing the overall risk to the public as low but emphasizing the seriousness of the cluster.
