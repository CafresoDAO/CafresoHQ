# 04: Diagnostic Methods for Hantavirus

Diagnosing Hantavirus Pulmonary Syndrome (HPS) can be challenging because its early [[03-symptoms-and-clinical-progression|symptoms]]—such as fever, muscle aches, and headaches—are non-specific and resemble other viral illnesses. A clinician must first suspect hantavirus, often based on the patient's history of potential rodent exposure, before ordering specific tests.

The definitive diagnosis is made through blood tests that detect the body's immune response to the virus. The primary method is an enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay (ELISA) which looks for IgM antibodies, indicating an acute infection. Other lab tests may be used to rule out conditions with similar symptoms.
