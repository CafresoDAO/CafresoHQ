# 12: Long-Term Survivor Outcomes

Survival from the acute phase of Hantavirus Pulmonary Syndrome (HPS) caused by the [[02-andes-virus-transmission|Andes virus]] does not always equate to a full recovery. Evidence from the 2026 outbreak suggests that many survivors experience persistent long-term physical and mental health problems, a condition that has been informally termed "long hantavirus."

During the immediate convalescence period, survivors frequently become polyuric (producing large volumes of dilute urine) and may show signs of mildly impaired renal function, though they typically improve rapidly afterwards. The existence of longer-term sequelae underscores the need for follow-up care beyond the acute [[05-treatment-and-patient-management|treatment and patient management]] phase to address the full impact of the infection.
