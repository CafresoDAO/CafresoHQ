# Crew vs. Passenger Risk Profiles and Outcomes

While public health organizations like the WHO and CDC have confirmed the Andes virus outbreak on the MV Hondius in 2026, reporting approximately nine confirmed cases and three deaths by mid-May, the available public data does not differentiate between crew members and passengers. This lack of specific data makes a direct comparison of infection rates and outcomes between the two groups impossible at this stage.

However, it is critical to consider their distinct risk profiles:

*   **Crew:** Likely faced prolonged exposure due to their work environment and living quarters. Responsibilities in cleaning, food service, and engineering could have brought them into closer contact with the virus's source or vector.
*   **Passengers:** Had more varied itineraries and potentially less consistent exposure, though shared dining and excursion activities still posed a risk.

Understanding this disparity is crucial for refining future public health responses on cruise ships. Further investigation into the specific roles and locations of those infected is needed to create a complete epidemiological picture. This remains a significant gap in the publicly available information regarding the [[01-initial-outbreak-summary|initial outbreak]].
