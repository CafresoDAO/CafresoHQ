# 05 - Treatment and Patient Management

There is no specific antiviral treatment or vaccine available for Andes virus infection. According to public health agencies like the CDC and ECDC, patient management is primarily supportive care.

The main goal of treatment is to manage the patient's symptoms, particularly the severe respiratory issues associated with Hantavirus Pulmonary Syndrome (HPS). This often requires hospitalization and may include:

- Oxygen therapy to maintain adequate blood oxygen levels.
- Mechanical ventilation in cases of severe respiratory failure.
- Careful management of fluid levels and blood pressure.

Given the rapid progression from initial [[03-symptoms-and-clinical-progression|symptoms]] to severe disease, early [[04-diagnostic-methods|diagnosis]] and prompt admission to an intensive care unit are critical to improving patient outcomes. The high case fatality rate underscores the severity of the illness and the importance of immediate medical intervention.
