# 18: International Health Organization Coordination

The 2026 hantavirus outbreak on the MV Hondius cruise ship prompted a coordinated response from major international health organizations. The World Health Organization (WHO) was formally notified of the cluster of severe acute respiratory illness on May 2, 2026, which initiated a multi-agency collaboration to manage the crisis involving passengers and crew from 23 different countries.

Key agencies involved included:
*   **World Health Organization (WHO):** Served as the central coordinating body, disseminating information through its Disease Outbreak News platform and guiding the global public health strategy.
*   **U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC):** Responded by issuing a Health Alert Network (HAN) notice and providing critical expertise on the Andes virus, which was identified as the causative agent.
*   **European Centre for Disease Prevention and Control (ECDC):** Actively monitored the situation, as the vessel was Dutch-flagged and carried passengers from numerous EU/EEA countries.

This international cooperation was fundamental to the overall [[07-public-health-response-and-epidemiology]] and was crucial for implementing the complex [[16-international-repatriation-and-quarantine-protocols]].
