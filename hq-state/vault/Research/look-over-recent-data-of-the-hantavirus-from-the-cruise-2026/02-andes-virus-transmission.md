# Andes Virus: Unique Transmission Characteristics

The Andes virus (ANDV) is a type of hantavirus with a critical distinction: it is the only known hantavirus capable of person-to-person transmission. This is a key factor in understanding the [[01-initial-outbreak-summary|MV Hondius outbreak]].

While the primary transmission route is still through inhalation of aerosolized particles from infected rodent droppings, saliva, or urine, the secondary human-to-human route makes it particularly dangerous in close-contact settings.

### Person-to-Person Spread
- **Mechanism:** Occurs via close contact with an infected individual, especially in enclosed spaces.
- **Infectious Window:** Evidence suggests the period of highest infectivity is short, peaking around the day the infected person develops a fever.
