# SNS DAO Launch Case Studies and Success Metrics

## Participation Rate Variations Across SNS DAOs

Research from "Democracy for DAOs: An Empirical Study of Decentralized Governance" reveals significant participation disparities:

- **High engagement DAOs** (Nuance, ICPanda, ICGhost): 74-77% participation
- **Lower engagement** (Kinic): ~54% participation despite ~5000 neurons
- Correlation: smaller neuron counts often show higher engagement rates

## Notable SNS Launches on Internet Computer

### OpenChat (3e3x2-xyaaa-aaaaq-aaalq-cai)
- Positioned as "decentralized chat app governed by the people for the people"
- One of the most prominent SNS DAOs in the ecosystem
- Strong community engagement through meme features and social utility

### Dragginz (MMORPG)
- Built by former Neopets developers (Adam & Donna Powell)
- Adopted SNS framework for fully on-chain adventure game
- Gaming sector use case demonstrating SNS versatility

### Kinic (Search Engine)
- Web3-native search engine with ~5000 neurons
- Lower participation rate suggests challenges in voter engagement at scale
- Pursuing technical automation and multi-chain indexers

## Success Factors Observed

1. **Clear token utility** - OpenChat's social features drive usage
2. **Community size vs engagement trade-off** - larger DAOs face participation challenges
3. **Product-market fit before launch** - Dragginz leveraged founder reputation
4. **Ongoing governance activity** - Kinic's regular technical proposals maintain momentum

## Failure Signals

- **SNS-1 controversies** - early launch bugs damaged trust, though recovered
- **Low participation in large DAOs** - dilution of individual voting power
- **Lack of token utility beyond governance** - relates to [[token-utility-and-value-capture]]
